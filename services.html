Our Services

Complete Engineering. Every Trade.

Capabilities

From Concept to Commissioning, Across Every Discipline

Parsec Lab is a multidisciplinary engineering firm that covers every trade required to deliver a data center project. From mechanical and electrical systems to controls, architecture, and custom cooling equipment, our integrated approach ensures that every discipline works in concert from day one.

Unlike traditional MEP-only firms, Parsec Lab provides architecture, mechanical, electrical, controls, and equipment development under one roof. This eliminates coordination gaps between separate consultants, reduces change orders during construction, and accelerates project delivery from concept through commissioning.

Mechanical Electrical Controls & Automation Architecture BIM Coordination Equipment Development

Mechanical Engineering

Precision HVAC and cooling system design for mission-critical environments. Our mechanical engineers specialize in chilled water plants, direct liquid cooling, economizer strategies, and heat rejection systems. We design for the full range from edge deployments to 60+ MW hyperscale campuses.

  • Chilled water plant design and optimization
  • Direct liquid cooling (DLC) integration for AI/HPC
  • Air-side and water-side economizer strategies
  • Hot and cold aisle containment
  • Fire protection and suppression systems
  • Piping design and hydraulic analysis

Electrical Engineering

Power distribution engineered for uptime. From medium-voltage switchgear to rack-level PDUs, our electrical team designs redundant power architectures across Tier I through Tier IV. Every scenario is modeled: dual-feed redundancy, generator run-time analysis, arc flash studies, and power quality optimization.

  • Medium-voltage switchgear and transformer sizing
  • UPS system design and battery energy storage
  • Generator paralleling and fuel systems
  • Busway routing and PDU specifications
  • Arc flash analysis and protection coordination
  • Renewable energy integration

Controls & Automation

Intelligent infrastructure starts with smart controls. We design and implement BMS, DCIM, and custom telemetry platforms that provide real-time performance data, automated fault response, and trend analysis. Whether on-site or remote, operators get full visibility.

  • Building Management System (BMS) design
  • Data Center Infrastructure Management (DCIM)
  • PLC programming and sequence of operations
  • Remote monitoring dashboards
  • Automated cooling and power control sequences
  • Integration with third-party platforms

Architecture

Data center architecture is more than floor plans. Our architects optimize facility layouts for operational workflow, thermal management, structural loading, and regulatory compliance. From greenfield campuses to brownfield retrofits, we deliver designs that work for both people and machines.

  • Facility master planning and site layout
  • Building envelope and structural coordination
  • Raised floor and slab-on-grade configurations
  • Regulatory compliance and permitting
  • Space planning for IT, mechanical, and electrical rooms
  • Modular and premanufactured building design

BIM Coordination

Every cable tray, cooling unit, and structural member in one coordinated model. Our BIM workflows drive clash-free construction, faster installations, and real-time cost control. We maintain digital twins that support both construction and ongoing facility management.

  • 3D coordination across all disciplines
  • Clash detection and resolution
  • Construction-ready documentation
  • Digital twin development and maintenance
  • Quantity takeoffs and cost tracking
  • As-built documentation and handover

Cooling Equipment Development

Beyond design services, Parsec Lab engineers and produces custom cooling equipment. From concept to production, our team develops air handling units, rear-door heat exchangers, coolant distribution units, and modular cooling systems purpose-built for the extreme demands of high-density data centers.

  • Custom Air Handling Units (150-300 kW)
  • Rear-door heat exchangers
  • Coolant Distribution Units (CDUs)
  • Modular cooling systems
  • Prototype development and testing
  • Production-ready manufacturing documentation
